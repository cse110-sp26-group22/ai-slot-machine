# Results Analysis

## Overview

Five AI-generated scripts (5, 19, 22, 27, 35) were evaluated based on the differences observed between Step 1 and Step 2 outputs, with a focus on UI improvements, visual engagement, graphics, and audio enhancements.

From this evaluation, scripts **5, 22, and 27** were selected for further analysis, while **19 and 35** were excluded due to minimal noticeable improvements.

---

## Comparison Chart

| Script | UI Changes (Step 1 → Step 2) | Visual & Graphics Quality | Audio Enhancements | Overall Impact | Decision |
|--------|------------------------------|----------------------------|--------------------|----------------|----------|
| 5      | Significant improvements in layout and interactivity | Strong visuals and engaging graphics | Noticeable audio improvements | High | Selected |
| 19     | Minimal to no noticeable changes | Weak or unchanged visuals | Little to no improvement | Low | Not Selected |
| 22     | Clear UI refinement and better structure | High-quality visuals and graphics | Improved and more engaging audio | High | Selected |
| 27     | Strong UI with good usability, but little to no changes | Visually compelling and dynamic | Good audio experience | Medium | Selected |
| 35     | Very limited differences | Basic visuals with little enhancement | Minimal changes | Low | Not Selected |

---

## Conclusion

The decision to move forward with scripts **5, 22, and 27** was based on their ability to clearly demonstrate improvement and effectiveness through multiple dimensions—UI, visuals, and audio.

Scripts **19 and 35** were not selected because their limited differences between iterations made them less useful for deeper analysis. The lack of meaningful changes reduced their value in understanding what contributes to more effective AI-generated outputs.

Focusing on the stronger candidates allows for more insightful analysis into the elements that drive engagement and quality in AI-generated scripts.
